A few habits, if you want sharper feedback

Optional

Skip straight to your interview if you'd rather — or take twenty seconds on the four habits that turn small talk into real practice:

Paste the whole prompt. The exact wording keeps the grading rubric consistent — don't summarize it first.
Answer like it's real. Genuine answers get genuine feedback. Rushed or sarcastic gets the same back.
Redirect it out loud. Too easy? Say "push harder" or "be more skeptical" — it adjusts immediately.

03

Your pipeline lives in your Drive — not a browser tab

Optional for now

Skip this for now if you'd rather start practicing immediately — come back to it once you're actually applying to firms, not before your first round. A recruiting cycle runs for months, across every device you own, so when that time comes, keep your pipeline somewhere a cleared cache can't touch:

  1. Open tracker/tracker.xlsx from your download.
  2. Upload it to Google Drive, then right-click it → Open with → Google Sheets — that conversion step matters, since it turns the file into a real editable Sheet, not just a preview.
  3. Done — that's now your tracker, permanently.

Prefer to skip Google Drive entirely? The file opens fine directly in Excel or Numbers — no account needed — and you can always move it to Sheets later.

Drive shows a brief "converting" spinner during that step — that's expected, not an error.

Your interview scores get their own tracker right in the app, since that's quick, personal data — but a pipeline you'll update for months, from more than one device, needs real spreadsheet power that outlasts anything stored only in browser storage.
