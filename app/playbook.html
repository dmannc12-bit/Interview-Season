Positioning

Figure out where to aim before you start applying everywhere — split your list into three tiers, then let the prompt below fill each one in.

Reach

Firms where your profile is a stretch — GPA, school tier, or experience below their typical bar. Apply, but don't build your whole strategy around them.

Target

Firms where you're a realistic, competitive candidate. This tier should get the majority of your time and tailoring effort.

Safety

Firms where you comfortably clear the bar. Not a consolation prize — a real offer here changes your leverage everywhere else.

Assessments & HireVue

The gate most prep guides skip — and the one that eliminates applicants before a human ever reads their resume.

Numerical & logical reasoning

These are timed at roughly 60-90 seconds per question. Keep scratch paper next to you, round aggressively rather than computing exact decimals, and if you're stuck for more than 20 seconds, skip it and come back — one unanswered question costs less than three rushed wrong ones.

HireVue / recorded video

Set up and dress exactly as you would for a live interview — the camera reads sloppiness the same way a person would. Look at the camera lens, not your own preview window. Keep answers to roughly 90 seconds, and do one silent practice take before recording for real so the first take isn't your first take.

Situational judgment tests

There's no universal "correct" personality to fake. Answer consistently with how you'd actually behave under pressure — graders flag extreme "always" or "never" answers as inconsistent more often than they reward them.
