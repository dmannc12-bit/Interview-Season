The rubric

A strong finance bullet does four things. Miss any one and it reads as filler.

  1. 01
    Leads with a strong action verb.Built, led, analyzed, negotiated — never "responsible for" or "helped."
  2. 02
    Names the specific method or tool."In Excel," "using a DCF," "via a SQL query" — the how, not just the what.
  3. 03
    Quantifies the result.A percentage, a dollar amount, a time saved, a count — something a reader can't dismiss as vague self-praise.
  4. 04
    Fits on one line, no fluff.If it wraps, cut adjectives first — "successfully," "effectively," and "various" carry zero information.

Bullet formulas

Nine patterns covering the situations most finance resumes actually need to describe.

[verb] + [task] + [method] + [quantified result] Built a 3-statement operating model in Excel to forecast working capital needs, reducing forecast variance by 18%.
[verb] + [analysis type] + [scope] + [decision impact] Analyzed 40+ comparable transactions to benchmark acquisition multiples, informing a $12M bid recommendation.
[verb] + [process] + [method] + [efficiency gained] Automated the weekly reporting process using Excel macros, cutting turnaround time from 6 hours to 45 minutes.
[verb] + [stakeholder] + [deliverable] + [outcome] Presented an investment thesis to a 12-person client committee, securing approval for a $2M allocation.
[verb] + [dataset/scale] + [technique] + [finding] Mined 5 years of transaction data in SQL to identify seasonal revenue drivers, reshaping the Q4 forecast model.
[verb] + [team/role] + [project] + [deadline result] Led a 4-person team through a 6-week valuation project, delivering the final model two days ahead of deadline.
[verb] + [cost/revenue lever] + [method] + [$ impact] Identified $340K in redundant vendor contracts through a spend audit, recommending consolidation to finance leadership.
[verb] + [risk/control] + [method] + [outcome] Built a covenant-compliance tracker across 15 credit facilities, flagging two breaches before the reporting deadline.
[verb] + [research scope] + [comparison] + [conclusion] Benchmarked 10 peer companies' capital structures, concluding the target was under-levered relative to its sector.

Kill-list

If any of these appear on your resume, the bullet is describing an obligation, not an accomplishment.

Responsible for Helped with Worked on Duties included Assisted in Participated in Team player Detail-oriented Hardworking Various tasks

Division-specific patterns

The same experience reads differently depending on what a screener is scanning for.

IB Emphasize modeling depth, deal exposure, and accuracy under deadline pressure. Numbers should signal rigor: model size, comps count, hours compressed.
S&T Emphasize speed of judgment, market awareness, and quantitative pricing skill. Favor bullets that show decisions made under time pressure, not just analysis produced.
PE / AM Emphasize investment judgment and diligence depth over speed. A single well-reasoned thesis outweighs volume — show how you evaluated, not just what you produced.
Corp Fin Emphasize forecasting accuracy, process ownership, and cross-functional collaboration. Show you can own a recurring deliverable end to end.
