Networking & Outreach Engine

For most students — especially non-targets — a referral from a real conversation opens more doors than another application. This is that process, systematized: find, reach, chat, follow up, track.

Find

Build a target list before you send a single message.

  1. 01Search LinkedIn for "[your school] + works at [firm]" — alumni two-plus years out are more likely to reply than someone your exact age.
  2. 02Use your school's career center alumni database — most have one, and it's underused, which means less competition for replies.
  3. 03Check your school's LinkedIn "Alumni" tool, filtered by target firm and industry — it surfaces people the search bar misses.
  4. 04Attend firm-hosted info sessions and virtual events — note names of anyone who speaks, they're pre-warmed for outreach.
  5. 05Log every name in the Contacts tab of your tracker the moment you find them — a list that only lives in your head evaporates.

Reach out

These are the messages that actually get replies — a cold email, a LinkedIn note, a thank-you, and a referral ask — because a specific message gets answered and a mass-blasted one gets ignored. Pick one, fill in the fields, copy, and send.

Chat

20-30 minutes, mostly them talking. The goal is a real conversation, not an interrogation.

Do

  • Come with 3-4 specific questions about their path, not "any advice for me?"
  • Ask about their actual day-to-day, not just how they landed the job.
  • Let them talk — aim for roughly 70/30, them to you.
  • End by asking who else they'd suggest you speak with.

Don't

  • Ask for a referral in the first conversation.
  • Ask anything a 30-second search would answer.
  • Run over the time you asked for.
  • Make it purely transactional — show real interest in their path.

Questions worth asking

  • —What does a typical week look like for you at [firm]?
  • —What made you choose [firm] over other offers you had?
  • —What's something about the desk or group that surprised you once you were actually in it?
  • —In your view, what separates a strong first-year analyst from an average one?
  • —Is there anyone else you'd suggest I speak with?

Follow up

The cadence is what turns one good conversation into a referral — most students stop after the thank-you note.

DAY 0 Coffee chat The conversation happens.
DAY 1 Thank-you note Reference one specific thing they said — proves you were listening.
WEEK 2-3 Value touch Share something relevant, or a short update on your own progress.
WEEK 5-6 Referral ask Direct and low-pressure, once your application is actually live.
ONGOING Close the loop Tell them the outcome either way — this is who you become a known quantity to.

Track

Every contact you find, every chat you run, and every follow-up due date lives in the Contacts tab of your tracker — not in your memory or a screenshot folder.
