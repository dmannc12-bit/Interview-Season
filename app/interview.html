Interviewer protocols

Framework skeletons

Not full explainers — the skeleton to hang your own answer on. Memorize the shape, not a script.

Walk me through your resume
  • 01Open (10s): one line on who you are, present tense.
  • 02Body (45s): 2-3 experiences in what/why/result form, one throughline.
  • 03Close (10s): tie the throughline directly to why this role, this firm, now.
  • 04Hand-off: end on a beat they can follow up on, not a dead stop.
Why banking
  • 01The spark: one specific moment that got you interested — not "I've always loved finance."
  • 02The test: what you did to confirm it — classes, deals you followed, coffee chats.
  • 03The fit: why your strengths specifically suit the work, not the lifestyle.
  • 04The now: why this firm, this group, this cycle.
Stock pitch skeleton
  • 01Recommendation: long/short, one line why.
  • 02Thesis: 2-3 differentiated points — not consensus takes.
  • 03Catalyst & valuation: what makes it play out now, at what target price.
  • 04Risk: steelman the bear case yourself before they do.
Brainteaser approach
  • 01Restate the problem out loud, then state your assumptions explicitly.
  • 02Break it into a structure: segment, estimate, multiply.
  • 03Sanity-check the final number against something you already know.
  • 04Never go silent — narrate your thinking the whole way through.
Greatest weakness
  • 01Pick a real weakness that's actually true — not a disguised strength.
  • 02Give one concrete moment it showed up, briefly.
  • 03Name the specific step you took to address it.
  • 04Show what's measurably different now, and what's still in progress.
Three-statement linkage
  • 01Net income flows from the income statement into the CFS and retained earnings.
  • 02The CFS adjusts net income for non-cash items (D&A) and working-capital changes.
  • 03Ending cash and capex flow into the balance sheet — cash and PP&E.
  • 04Interest from the debt schedule flows back up to the income statement.
DCF walkthrough
  • 01Project unlevered free cash flow, typically 5 years.
  • 02Discount each year's FCF at the WACC.
  • 03Terminal value (Gordon growth or exit multiple), discounted back to present.
  • 04Sum the PVs for Enterprise Value, then bridge to equity value.
Comps walkthrough
  • 01Screen a peer set: same sector, size, geography, growth/margin profile.
  • 02Pull EV and equity value for each peer; normalize for one-offs.
  • 03Calculate EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, and P/E — take the median, not the mean.
  • 04Apply the peer median multiple to the target's own metric for implied value.
Quick / paper LBO
  • 01Entry EV = entry multiple × entry EBITDA; sources = debt + sponsor equity.
  • 02Project EBITDA growth and debt paydown over the hold period.
  • 03Exit EV = exit multiple × exit-year EBITDA.
  • 04Exit equity = exit EV − remaining debt; IRR/MOIC follow from entry vs. exit.
M&A / accretion-dilution
  • 01Combine acquirer and target net income; add synergies, net of tax.
  • 02Net new interest expense against foregone interest on cash used.
  • 03Combine share counts, plus any new shares issued in a stock deal.
  • 04Compare pro forma EPS to the acquirer's standalone EPS — accretive or dilutive.

Curated question set

A lean set worth rehearsing out loud, not a 500-question bank. Depth beats breadth here.

Behavioral

  • Tell me about a time you led a team through a setback.
  • Tell me about a time you disagreed with someone senior to you.
  • Tell me about your biggest failure and what you changed after it.
  • Tell me about a time you had to manage multiple priorities under a tight deadline.
  • Why investment banking — and why not consulting, PE, or tech?
  • Why this firm specifically?

Technical — Accounting

  • Walk me through the three financial statements.
  • If depreciation increases by $10, what happens across all three statements?
  • What's the difference between cash and accrual accounting?

Technical — Valuation & Deals

  • Walk me through a DCF.
  • What are the three main valuation methodologies, and when would you use each?
  • Walk me through a basic LBO.
  • What makes a good LBO candidate?

Markets & Fit

  • Pitch me a stock you like.
  • What's a deal you've been following, and what do you think of it?
  • What's happening in markets right now that you find interesting?
